Aggeliki Teligiannidou

Aggeliki Teligiannidou is a graduate of the speech therapy department of the School of Health and Welfare Professions of the Technological Educational Institute of Epirus in Greece. As part of her diploma thesis, she has conducted a pilot application of the TOPL 1 test in the Greek population. She has been working in the private sector as a Speech Therapist since 2007. Since 2008 she has been running the Speech and Language Therapy Center with Maria Tsimourdaga. She was an external scientific associate in the cochlear implantation department of the university clinic of the Ahepa Hospital in Thessaloniki, from 2008 to 2012. In 2014, the collaboration with the Occupational Therapy Center began, which led to the creation of the Center for Special Therapies "Simeio". She is specialized in Lidcombe. methods, Neurofunctional Reorganization, Intensive Interaction, Palin PCI, P.E.K.S.E. (Level 1). She is a certified sponsor of the Phonetics and Phonological Evolution Test (P.S.L., 1995), the weighted language assessment tool Logometer as well as the Developmental Profile Communication Model: Model of Cycles. She participated in the workshop (25 hours) on: Problems that occur in the comprehension and production of oral speech, in relation to the particular characteristics of the Greek language. Their evaluation, diagnosis and treatment. (Levanti Irene, 2014). She has attended numerous seminars such as Stuttering in childhood, Evaluation – Therapeuti Intervention of Voice Disorders, , Speech Therapy Massage, Vision and Learning, Vision and Autism, Children's Tracing , Messy Play: Sensory play as a means of development etc. Attends the Internationally Accredited Hanen Program for Early Childhood Professionals: Hanen "Teacher Talk" (Center for Training and Lifelong Learning) of the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA) Finally, she has published in e-poster form at the 13th International Conference on Cochlear Implants (Munich, Germany 18-21 June 2014) her research paper on "Comparison of speech discrimination and comprehension of cochlear implant users: computer produced speech versus live voice" She continues to be constantly updated and educated on new scientific methods and developments related to the science of speech therapy.
