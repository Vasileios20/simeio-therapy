Welcome to the Center for Special Treatments "Simeio"

Four therapists starting from different points (different fields) joined their paths. Driven by their love for their fellow man, vision and common values, they fulfilled their dream to create a space that promotes the enlightenment of the individual. Our main concern is the respect of each patient and his uniqueness, the inclusion of all specialties, the individual and the family in the therapeutic journey.

Our Team

Maria

Maria Tsimourdaga is a graduate of St. Kliment Ohridski University, Sofia, Bulgaria. She holds a higher education degree in Speech Therapy and a second specialty in Hearing and Speech Rehabilitation. She also holds postgraduate studies in Special Education - Speech and Language Therapy specializing in Communication Disorders in Development. She has been working as a speech therapist since 2000 and since 2002 she maintains the Speech and Language Therapy Center. From September 2002 to June 2004 she worked at the Association of Parents and Guardians of Deaf and Hard of Hearing Children of Central Macedonia while at the same time she was an external scientific associate in the cochlear implantation department of the University Clinic "AHEPA" in Thessaloniki. In 2014, the collaboration with the Occupational Therapy Center "Exelixis" began, which led to the creation of the Center for Special Therapies Point. In 2022 she created the Center for Special Therapies "Simeio", after many years of collaboration with the Occupational Therapy Center "Exelixis". She has specialized in the methods of Neurofunctional Reorganization, Intensive Interaction, P.E.S.E. (Level-1), MFT (Myofunctional Therapy) as well as the Multisensory Method of Phonological Awareness, Reading and Writing. She is a certified sponsor of the Vocal and Phonological Progression Test (P.S.L., 1995), the School Readiness Test A Test as well as the weighted language assessment tool Logometer. She participated in the workshop (25 hours) on: Problems that occur in the comprehension and production of oral speech, in relation to the particular characteristics of the Greek language. Their evaluation, diagnosis and treatment. (Levanti Irene, 2014). She has sufficient professional knowledge in Dyslexia – Assessment and Intervention Techniques as well as Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)- (Papanastasiou 2021). She has attended numerous seminars - workshops such as "Speech Problems: Assessment - Differential Diagnosis - Treatment ", THERAPLAY, "Stuttering in childhood", Vision and Learning, Vision and Autism , Cochlear Implants, Speech Therapy Massage, The Child with Sensory Processing Difficulties, Children's Tracing, Messy Play, Music Therapy, Executive Functions, MnemonicProcessing and its Contribution to the Daily Functioning of the Child, etc. She attends the Internationally Accredited Hanen Program for Early Childhood Professionals: Hanen "Teacher Talk". She has also taken part in educational - experiential seminars of the Gestalt Foundation. It continues to be constantly updated and educated on new scientific methods and developments related to the science of speech therapy.

Gelly

As part of her diploma thesis, she has conducted a pilot application of the TOPL 1 test in the Greek population. She has been working in the private sector as a Speech Therapist since 2007. Since 2008 she has been running the Speech and Language Therapy Center with Maria Tsimourdaga. She was an external scientific associate in the cochlear implantation department of the university clinic of the Ahepa Hospital in Thessaloniki, from 2008 to 2012. In 2014, the collaboration with the Occupational Therapy Center began, which led to the creation of the Center for Special Therapies Point. She is specialized in Lidcombe. methods, Neurofunctional Reorganization, Intensive Interaction, Palin PCI, P.E.K.S.E. (Level 1). She is a certified sponsor of the Phonetics and Phonological Evolution Test (P.S.L., 1995), the weighted language assessment tool Logometer as well as the Developmental Profile Communication Model: Model of Cycles. She participated in the workshop (25 hours) on: Problems that occur in the comprehension and production of oral speech, in relation to the particular characteristics of the Greek language. Their evaluation, diagnosis and treatment. (Levanti Irene, 2014). She has attended numerous seminars such as Stuttering in childhood, Evaluation – Therapeutic Intervention of Voice Disorders, , Speech Therapy Massage, Vision and Learning, Vision and Autism, Children's Tracing , Messy Play: Sensory play as a means of development etc. Attends the Internationally Accredited Hanen Program for Early Childhood Professionals: Hanen "Teacher Talk" (Center for Training and Lifelong Learning (K.E.DI.VI.M.) of the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA) Finally, she has published in e-poster form at the 13th International Conference on Cochlear Implants (Munich, Germany 18-21 June 2014) her research paper on "Comparison of speech discrimination and comprehension of cochlear implant users: computer produced speech versus live voice" She continues to be constantly updated and educated on new scientific methods and developments related to the science of speech therapy.

Elena

Elena Koundourou studied occupational therapy at the Technological Educational Institute of Athens and completed her studies at the Karolinska Institute of Stockholm, where she also specialized in neurological rehabilitation. From 2000 to 2004 she worked as an external collaborator in a rehabilitation center, special therapies offices and home treatments, while at the same time she began to specialize in pediatric occupational therapy and even more specifically in autism. From 2005 to 2008 she was in charge of the occupational therapy department at a large rehabilitation center. In 2005 she created the occupational therapy center "Exelixis" which maintains to this day with main interest the rehabilitation of neurological and developmental disorders in children. In 2014, the occupational therapy center "Exelixis" co-located and permanently collaborated with the Speech and Language Therapy Center, thus improving the benefits to its customers. This collaboration developed and matured, as a result of which the two centers were united under a new name and the creation of the center for special therapies "Simeio". In addition to the above, she is a supervisor of students and graduates of occupational therapy, a member of the interdisciplinary team of many kindergartens in Thessaloniki and a member of the Panhellenic Association of Occupational Therapists. She also collaborates with the Metropolitan College of Thessaloniki, in various roles within the department of occupational therapy, such as that of teacher of courses, head teacher of first year and responsible practiceIng. She is specialized in Sensory Integration, Vision, PECS and TEEACH methods, Social Histories, Behavior Management methods for people with disabilities, Motor Learning, PNF, Functional Tone Management, Animal Assisted Therapy, Circus Therapy and many other methods. In 2020 she began her master's degree ( MSc) in rehabilitation with a view to neurodevelopmental pediatric rehabilitation at Oxford Brooks University , England. From the beginning of her career until now she has not stopped educating herself so that the times do not overtake her and she faces occupational therapy with unquenchable passion, love and responsibility.

Katerina

Katerina Gesou is a graduate of the BSc Occupational Therapy, Queen Margaret University and the Department of Physical Education and Sports Science (TEFAA) specializing in Special Physical Education. She continued her specialization with two master's degrees. The first comes from the Postgraduate Program "Kinesiology" of the Department of Physical Education and Sport Science of Serres where she dealt with the effect of Electrical Stimulation on the fatigue of the muscles of the thenar while the second from the Special Inclusive Education European University of Cyprus where she dealt with the Evaluation of the understanding of the emotions of others through Psychomotor by children with Autism Spectrum Disorder. At the level of training, she has completed the training of Sensory Integration while her personal portfolio includes the Seminar on learning infant swimming, the seminar on Psychomotor- Music-Kinetic Education of EKPA and the seminar of the Hellenic Society Halliwic. Swimming and Therapy for People with Special needs. She has also attended the 216-hour Professional Yoga Learning Program specializing in children's yoga. She is specialized in PECS and TEEACH methods, Social Histories, Behavioral Management methods for people with disabilities, Motor Learning and Cognitive Orientation Approach in the daily execution of a CO-OP (Cognitive Orientation to daily Occupational Performance) project. From 2005 to 2018 she worked as an external collaborator in kindergartens and structures for people with disabilities, organizing psychomotor and adapted physical activity programs for children with difficulties in movement. Since 2007 she has been delivering personalized programs of Kinetic Intervention, Occupational Therapy and Hydrotherapy at home in collaboration with private and public swimming pools of the city and from 2012-2017 she provided educational parallel support to children with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Down syndrome, while at the same time she was an external partner in a private Physiotherapy Center where she began to specialize in pediatric Occupational Therapy. From 2017-2022 she worked in large Rehabilitation Centers framing the Department of Adult Occupational Therapy, while at the same time she was in charge of their respective Pediatric Department. Since 2021 she has been working as an occupational therapist at the Pediatric Therapy Center "Excelixi" of Elena Koundourou while also collaborating with the Metropolitan College of Thessaloniki as a teacher. The collaboration with Elena Koundourou developed and matured, resulting in the creation of the Center for Special Therapies "Simeio". From the beginning of her career until now she has not stopped training, while her unstoppable passion and love for Occupational Therapy remain the driving force to continue her Lifelong Education.
