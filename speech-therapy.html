Speech Therapy

Speech therapy is the science that deals with speech, speaking voice and chewing-swallowing disorders in children and adults, whatever the cause of these disorders: neurological, developmental or functional. It aims at the development of extra-verbal and verbal communication, the clarity of speech and voice and the speech production of the hard of hearing.

From the age of about eighteen months until the day the child goes to school, he first learns sounds, then words, and finally to make sentences. But if he does not learn to speak properly, when he later goes to school he will have difficulty writing properly. Many of the children who have learning difficulties at school age had speech problems in preschool. Fortunately, however, the reverse is not the case: It is not a rule that all children who have speech problems will also develop learning problems. This is because, when speech problems are detected very early, they can be overcome relatively easily with the help of a specialist.

For a parent to understand that their child has a learning problem, they must first join the learning process. However, from very early on there may be signs that the child will have difficulties in school. Already at the age of 2.5 years, unintelligible or difficult to understand speech is a sign that should concern parents, as it hides a complex phonological disorder that can affect his ability later to correctly decode syllables and words containing sounds that he had difficulty to produce earlier. Each person has their own pace of development. But there are certain time limits that, when exceeded, parents should seek expert advice.

Up to 12 months

  • Baby reacts to sounds
  • Makes eye contact
  • He recognizes his mother's voice
  • He smiles when you talk to him
  • It lives up to its name
  • He turns his head towards the source of the sounds
  • Has some basic vocabulary

Up to 2 years old

  • His vocabulary has 20-50 words
  • Obeys simple commands
  • Uses two words to form sentences
  • Uses various toys symbolically (e.g. feeding a baby, etc.)

Up to 3 years old

  • Answers questions
  • Makes simple suggestions
  • He can watch a dialogue
  • He likes fairy tales and follows them easily
  • He asks questions

Up to 4 years old

  • It answers all kinds of questions
  • It is understood even by people he does not know
  • His speech is closer to that of adults in terms of grammar and syntax
  • He narrates his experiences
