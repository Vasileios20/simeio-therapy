Speech Therapy

Speech therapy is the science that has as its subject the study, research and application of scientific knowledge about Human Communication (verbal or not), Speech (oral, written) Speech, Voice, and their disorders, as well as swallowing and feeding disorders. Speech therapy contributes to the improvement of the linguistic, cognitive and communication skills of people of all ages who face difficulties that hinder them in daily activities. Child development is a normal process of progression from an early and lower level of functionality to a more complex one, such as that of adults. Although each child has its own rate of language development, there are some linguistic milestones, i.e. "indicators" that serve to highlight the level of functioning of the child in relation to the expected levels based on his age in various areas of the individual's functionality. . The deviation from these milestones may be an indication that we should visit a speech therapist to have an assessment done and if required to proceed with the intervention through an individualized treatment program.

Occupational Therapy Robotics

Robotics is considered as an innovative activity that begins to enter dynamically and affect the educational process. Occupational therapy through robotics is an occupational therapy intervention program that uses the activity of robotics as a therapeutic agent. During this intervention the child enters the process of building his robot and programming it to do some process. The objectives of the program are for the child to increase his construction skills, reduce his impulse, develop and improve his executive functions such as problem solving and working memory and increase the ability to cooperate. It is aimed at children mainly with attention deficit disorder with or without hyperactivity, with learning difficulties, with difficulties in practice skills and with difficulties in social interaction. It is a very pleasant way of occupational therapy intervention that motivates children to try harder. Through creation, the feeling of fulfillment is created which increases the self-esteem of the child and gives quality and satisfaction to his life.

Occupational Therapy

Occupational therapy" as a term is derived from the words "work" and "therapy". It could be defined as therapy through work (activity) but also therapy for work (activity). Occupational therapy uses purposeful activity as a means of treatment. At the same time, it is concerned, through it, that the individual is able to engage in all the activities that have meaning for him adequately. It is a science of rehabilitation where in a holistic way it aims at the autonomy and functionality of the individual. Pediatric occupational therapy The pediatric field is one of the main fields of occupation of occupational therapists in Greece. Play is a very important activity for children and is used therapeutically in occupational therapy. The most important purpose of pediatric occupational therapy is the successful involvement of children in school activities (such as attendance in the classroom, recess, reading at home, etc.), in recreational activities (such as playing on the playground, cycling, team building play, family activities, etc.) and autonomy (food, clothing, personal hygiene, etc.) according to the typical standards of each age. The above can be achieved either by improving some skills of the child that are lacking (such as sensory processing, concentration, gross and fine motor skills, visual-motor coordination, self-confidence, self-organization, etc.), or by adapting the environment or the activity to level of the child.

Parental Counselling

Parental counseling takes place between the parents and the psychologist / psychotherapist to whom they are addressed. Counseling sessions discuss the issues that concern parents in relation to their child, family or their own role as parents. These issues may concern issues in a child's behavior, emotional difficulties, difficulties in socialization or difficulties faced by parents in their own role. Also, in the management of specific situations, such as a death or loss in the environment of the child, a serious illness that affects the daily life of the family and the child, the divorce of the parents or a traumatic event. Parental counseling depending on the situation may be the only intervention or be combined with the psychotherapy of the child or adolescent, as well as with other interventions, such as speech therapy and occupational therapy in the context of an overall therapeutic program. The aim in parental counseling is through the relationship that parents develop with the psychologist / psychotherapist to receive support in their role as parents, to get direction from the psychologist / psychotherapist in the management of specific issues, not through general advice but within a framework that takes into account the needs of the specific family and the specific child, to better understand the difficulties that their child may be facing and to give space to the development of a common attitude on the part of the parents that favors their cooperation. Depending on the circumstances, parental counseling may take place with one or both parents and the frequency of sessions may vary depending on the needs of the family and the therapist's assessment of the treatment program.
